Airlines struggle as COVID-19 soars

By Ben Scott | December 08, 2020

COVID-19 spurred nationwide lockdown orders in March, which caused the airline industry to experience an immidiate drop in busiuness. The industry is still recovering from these lockdown orders, and while the industry is slowly recovering as time progresses, any progress made towards recovery may be stifled by another increase in COVID-19 cases this winter season.

Air passengers recover as COVID-19 continues spread

Passenger numbers tanked when COVID-19 first spurred lockdowns beginnging in March and April. However, as COVID-19 continues spreading in the U.S., passengers are slowly beginning to resume flights, especially during the Thanksgiving season.

Fewer passengers per plane compared to before pandemic

Load factor, the technical term for passengers per plane, is currently less than 20% when compared to February 2020. Airlines rely on high load factor to make each flight profitable.

Missouri airports see drastic drop in overall traffic amidst COVID-19
